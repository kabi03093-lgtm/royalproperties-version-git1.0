Buying Guide

Documents you need before registering a plot

Royal Properties Team · 5 min read

Registration day goes smoothly when the paperwork is prepared well in advance. At minimum you'll need the sale deed draft, encumbrance certificate, patta or chitta extract, and identity proof for all parties.

If the plot is part of an approved layout, keep the DTCP approval copy on hand — sub-registrar offices increasingly ask for it during registration.

PAN card details are mandatory for property transactions above a certain value, and TDS may apply depending on the sale price.

Our documentation team prepares this checklist specific to each property before your registration date, so nothing is missing on the day.
